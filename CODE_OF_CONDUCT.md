# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

CreateForm Pro is committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We are dedicated to creating an environment where every person feels valued and respected, regardless of their background, identity, or experience level.

We pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, including:

- People of any age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation
- Beginners and experts
- Remote workers and in-office workers
- Community members and core maintainers

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

✅ **Be Respectful**
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be patient with others, especially newcomers
- Respect differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully

✅ **Be Collaborative**
- Work together toward shared goals
- Share knowledge and help others learn
- Give credit to others' contributions
- Build on each other's ideas

✅ **Be Professional**
- Focus on what's best for the community
- Assume good intentions in discussions
- Give credit where credit is due
- Be concise and clear in communication

✅ **Be Inclusive**
- Welcome new contributors
- Mentor less experienced members
- Amplify underrepresented voices
- Use inclusive language and examples

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

❌ **Harassment and Bullying**
- Offensive comments related to age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, derogatory comments
- Personal attacks or ad hominem arguments
- Intimidation or threats
- Stalking or following someone online
- Continued one-on-one contact after requests to stop
- Doxxing (publishing private information)

❌ **Discrimination**
- Refusing to work with someone
- Promoting harmful stereotypes
- Discriminatory language or jokes
- Exclusionary behavior based on protected characteristics

❌ **Abuse**
- Violent threats or violent language
- Advocating for violence against any person
- Abusive language toward others
- Repeated low-level harassment

❌ **Other Unacceptable Conduct**
- Spamming or self-promotion
- Sharing explicit or graphic content
- Disrupting ongoing discussions
- Going off-topic repeatedly
- Spreading misinformation

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you believe someone has violated this code of conduct, please report it by:

**Email:** conduct@CreateForm.dev  
**Form:** [Report Form Link]  
**Maintainer Direct:** Contact @maintainer on GitHub

**Include:**
- What happened (be specific)
- Where it happened (GitHub issue, PR, Discord, etc.)
- Who was involved
- When it happened
- Any evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)

**Your report will be:**
- ✓ Kept confidential
- ✓ Handled with respect
- ✓ Investigated promptly
- ✓ Met with appropriate action

### Our Commitment to Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing acceptable standards. They will take appropriate actions in response to violations, including:

1. **Verbal Warning** - For minor first offenses
2. **Public Apology** - When appropriate
3. **Temporary Ban** - 7-30 days from community
4. **Permanent Ban** - For serious or repeated violations

### Appeal Process

If you believe a moderation decision was unfair:

1. Email: conduct@CreateForm.dev
2. Specify why you disagree
3. We'll review with fresh eyes
4. Decision within 14 days

## Community Impact Guidelines

This is how we'll interpret and enforce:

### 1. Correction (Minor Issues)

**Behavior:** Use of inappropriate language, tone-deaf comments

**Action:**
- Private conversation with person
- Clear explanation of why problematic
- Opportunity to correct
- No public record

**Example:** Reminder about inclusive language

---

### 2. Warning (Moderate Issues)

**Behavior:** Repeated minor violations, single moderate violation

**Action:**
- Written warning
- May be made public
- Asked to stop
- Further action if continued

**Example:** Repeated dismissive comments → warning

---

### 3. Temporary Ban (Serious Issues)

**Behavior:** Serious violation, continuing after warning

**Action:**
- Banned from community interactions
- Duration: 7-30 days typically
- Can appeal after ban
- Publicly noted

**Example:** Harassment → 2-week ban → can rejoin if agrees to comply

---

### 4. Permanent Ban (Critical Issues)

**Behavior:** Extreme violations, dangerous threats, harassment campaigns

**Action:**
- Removed from community completely
- No appeal process
- Publicly noted
- Reported to authorities if needed

**Example:** Threats of violence → permanent ban → possible law enforcement

## Specific Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Someone Makes Offensive Comment

**What we do:**
1. Note the violation
2. Contact them privately
3. Explain why it's harmful
4. Ask for correction
5. If they apologize, consider resolved
6. If they refuse/repeat, escalate to warning

**What you should do:**
- Don't escalate publicly
- Report to maintainers
- Don't pile on the person

---

### Scenario 2: Someone Harasses Newcomer

**What we do:**
1. Immediately acknowledge the newcomer
2. Contact harasser privately
3. Make clear it's unacceptable
4. Warn of consequences
5. Monitor for continued behavior
6. Ban if continues

**What you should do:**
- Welcome the new person
- Report harassment
- Don't engage with harasser
- Show community supports newcomer

---

### Scenario 3: Heated Debate Between Contributors

**What we do:**
1. Let respectful debate happen
2. Step in if becomes disrespectful
3. Ask for refocus on ideas not people
4. End discussion if needed
5. Allow to resume if cooler

**What you should do:**
- Focus on ideas and code quality
- Disagree respectfully
- Criticize code, not person
- Accept feedback gracefully

---

### Scenario 4: Someone Makes Mistakes / Bad PR

**What we do:**
1. Provide constructive feedback
2. Explain improvements needed
3. Offer to help
4. Praise what's good
5. Collaborate to improve

**What you should do:**
- Be kind to new contributors
- Remember: everyone started somewhere
- Teach rather than judge
- Be encouraging

---

## Personal Boundaries

### Please Respect:

- **Time zones** - Don't expect instant responses
- **Work-life balance** - Maintainers have day jobs
- **Expertise** - Not everyone knows everything
- **Mental health** - Take breaks if needed
- **Privacy** - Don't share others' personal info

### Unacceptable Privacy Violations:

- ❌ Sharing someone's real name without permission
- ❌ Doxxing (publishing home address, phone, etc.)
- ❌ Sharing photos without consent
- ❌ Tagging someone repeatedly to get attention
- ❌ Contacting on personal channels after being asked not to

---

## Accommodations

### We Support:

✓ **Accessibility needs** - Alternative formats, captions, etc.  
✓ **Neurodiversity** - ADHD, autism, dyslexia, etc.  
✓ **Language barriers** - English non-native speakers  
✓ **Time zone differences** - Async communication  
✓ **Caregiving responsibilities** - Flexible participation  
✓ **Health situations** - Temporary reduced participation  

If you need accommodations or have accessibility needs, please let us know. We'll try our best to help.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community spaces (GitHub, Discord, email, etc.)
- Interactions between community members
- Representation of community at external events
- Discussions about CreateForm (even off-platform)

**It does NOT apply to:**
- Private conversations
- Interactions outside community context
- Personal political beliefs (unless in community context)

---

## Leadership Responsibilities

Community leaders will:

✓ **Model excellent behavior** - Be the example  
✓ **Remove harmful content** - Spam, threats, etc.  
✓ **Address violations** - Fair, consistent enforcement  
✓ **Recuse themselves** - If personally involved  
✓ **Maintain confidentiality** - Of reports and process  
✓ **Listen to community** - Feedback on enforcement
